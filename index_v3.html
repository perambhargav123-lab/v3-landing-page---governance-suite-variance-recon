Every month, your analysts export SAP data, paste it into Excel, manually calculate variances, write commentary, format a report, and email it to the CFO. For one entity. Then repeat 14 more times. Our 3-agent chain does the math in seconds, diagnoses root causes with AI, and writes a board-ready memo — with every number self-validated.

The problem

Workflow 02

Reconciliation Engine

Live — engine deployed

Close Readiness % — the metric nobody tracks but every CFO needs

Reconciliation consumes 60-70% of your month-end close window. Your team manually matches bank statements to GL, chases intercompany differences across 15 entities, and resolves AP/AR exceptions one by one. Our engine auto-matches 95%+ of transactions, classifies exceptions by materiality, suggests journal entries, and tells you exactly when it's safe to close.

The problem

30-40% of back-office finance cost is reconciliation (McKinsey). MIT/Stanford research shows AI can cut close cycles by 7.5 days. Dell's CFO deployed agents specifically for this. But no mid-market tool gives you a real-time "Close Readiness %" score. Ours does.

3-layer matching

Layer 1: Exact match (reference + amount + date). Layer 2: Fuzzy match (fuzzywuzzy — handles typos, date shifts, partial references, scoring 85%+ threshold). Layer 3: AI exception handling for the 1-5% that algorithms can't resolve. Modern Treasury's approach: deterministic engine handles 99%, AI handles 1%.

4 reconciliation types

Bank Reconciliation (BRS): bank statement vs GL cash book. AP Reconciliation: vendor statements vs GL payables. AR Reconciliation: customer ledger vs GL receivables. Intercompany: IC payables vs IC receivables across entities with currency translation and timing differences.

The Close Decision

Close Readiness % calculated in real-time. HOLD or CLOSE decision with risk score. Fast Close Path showing ordered steps to reach 95%+ readiness. JE suggestions with auto-post for low-risk items and review queue for material exceptions. SAP T-code FB50 integration.

Workflow 00

Data Foundation & Quality

Foundation layer

"You're only as good as the data you have" — David Kennedy, CFO Dell

Dell spent 2 years cleaning and standardizing data before deploying a single AI agent. Most companies skip this step and wonder why AI produces garbage. We don't let you skip it. Every engagement starts with a data health check — because an AI agent running on dirty data is worse than no AI at all.

Why this comes first

Garbage in, garbage out. If your chart of accounts isn't standardized, if GL descriptions are inconsistent, if cost centers map differently across entities — no AI agent can produce reliable output. This is Phase 1 of the Dell playbook: clean first, automate second.

What we check

SAP data extraction quality (FAGLL03H, FBL3N, KE30). Header row detection (not always Row 1). Duplicate line item detection. Debit/credit vs single balance format. Mixed P&L and Balance Sheet. GL code consistency across entities. Naming conventions. Currency uniformity.

Auto-cleaning engine

13 edge cases handled automatically: header not in Row 1, debit/credit format, single balance column, multiple periods, missing budget, BS/P&L mixed, duplicates, garbage naming, mixed currency (blocks analysis), unbalanced TB (blocks with override), negative reversals, exceptional items, format detection failure.

GL intelligence layer

SAP GL code mapping using standard ranges (4xxxxx revenue through 9xxxxx). 150+ keyword matching terms across 10 categories. Smart per-account-type sign normalization. Mapping memory saved to JSON — learns your chart of accounts and gets better each month.

Request data health check

This layer is built into every workflow — not sold separately. When you run variance analysis or reconciliation, the data foundation agent runs first automatically. Pure Python, zero AI, 100% accurate. Inspired by Dell CFO's 2-year data standardization initiative before any agent deployment.
